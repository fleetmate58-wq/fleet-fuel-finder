How to Reduce Fleet Fuel Costs in Australia

Proven strategies used by Australian fleet managers in 2026

📍

Buy fuel on the cheapest day

In most Australian cities, Tuesday and Wednesday are the cheapest days. Prices typically spike Thursday morning. Timing large fill-ups can save 5–10¢/L across your fleet.

🛞

Maintain correct tyre pressure

Under-inflated tyres increase rolling resistance, adding up to 3% to fuel consumption. A fleet of 20 vehicles could save $2,000–$4,000/year with weekly pressure checks.

📊

Use telematics to monitor driver behaviour

Harsh acceleration, excessive idling and speeding can inflate fuel costs by 15–25%. Fleet telematics data identifies the worst offenders and tracks improvement over time.

🗺️

Optimise routes to reduce empty kilometres

Route planning software can cut total fleet kilometres by 10–20%, directly reducing fuel spend. Prioritise removing unnecessary return-to-base trips.

⛽

Negotiate bulk fuel pricing or use fleet cards

Fleet fuel cards like FleetCard or Motorpass offer 3–6¢/L discounts at partner stations. For larger fleets, negotiate direct supply agreements with fuel distributors.

🔋

Consider hybrid or EV for suitable routes

For urban delivery and passenger vehicles doing under 200km/day, PHEVs and EVs can cut fuel costs by 50–80%. The 2026 FBT exemption for EVs under $89,332 makes the business case stronger.
